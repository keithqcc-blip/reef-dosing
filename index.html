Dissolve into about half the RODI, then top up to the batch volume. Keep the two parts in separate labelled jugs — concentrated, they precipitate into chalk if combined. Using anhydrous CaCl₂ instead of dihydrate? Randy says use about 20% less by volume.

Randy designs this to be dosed in equal volumes of both parts — that keeps calcium and alkalinity matched, so adjust the two together, never independently. Don't skip Part 3 (magnesium): add it each time you finish a gallon of both parts, or magnesium and sulfate slowly drift off.

Add salt to about half the RODI, dissolve and circulate, then top up — add the last bit slowly while testing. For a water change, set the target to match your tank, not 35. Measured a batch? Tell me what you got and I’ll re-fit the ratio.

Chemistry: Ca from CaCl₂·2H₂O (mass fraction 0.2726); alkalinity from soda ash (Na₂CO₃, 18.87 meq/g) or baking soda (NaHCO₃, 11.90 meq/g); 1 meq/L = 2.8 dKH; balance 7.14 ppm Ca per dKH. Recipe #1 = 500 g CaCl₂·2H₂O + 594 g baking soda baked to soda ash (≈375 g) per US gallon; Recipe #2 = 250 g CaCl₂·2H₂O + 297 g baking soda per US gallon. Always dose to a high-flow area and re-test. Source: Randy Holmes-Farley, Reefkeeping Magazine, Feb 2006.
